Discussion: Natural Selection (Darwin vs. Lamarck)

Darwin‚Äôs Natural Selection (Correct)

  • Variation exists in a population, such as short-neck and long-neck giraffes.
  • Differential survival occurs when some traits fit the environment better.
  • Those survivors reproduce more often than others.
  • Offspring inherit genetic traits from their parents.
  • Population-level change appears as trait frequencies shift over time.
  • Evolution happens across many generations, not in one instant.

Lamarck‚Äôs Acquired Traits (Incorrect)

  • Use and disuse proposes body parts change by frequent use.
  • Inheritance of acquired traits claims those lifetime changes pass to offspring.
  • This seems logical because organisms visibly adapt during life.
  • Genetics shows inherited information is transmitted through genes, not acquired body changes.

Connection to the Simulation

  • Darwin produces gradual graph change because selection and reproduction adjust trait frequencies step by step.
  • Lamarck produces instant transformation because it assumes immediate trait conversion within the same generation.
